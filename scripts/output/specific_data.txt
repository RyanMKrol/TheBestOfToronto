        <p><img src="http://www.blogto.com/upload/2014/11/20141117-poutine-toronto.jpg" width="590" height="393" alt="best poutine toronto"/>The best poutine in Toronto takes three humble, basic ingredients - fries, cheese curds, and gravy - and transmutes them into sloppy, melty, drunk-food magic. The Quebecois staple has become a bona fide food trend here in the city, colliding with everything from <a href="http://www.blogto.com/eat_drink/2015/02/where_to_eat_chicken_wing_poutine_in_toronto/">chicken wings</a> to <a href="http://www.blogto.com/eat_drink/2015/01/where_to_eat_japanese_poutine_in_toronto/">Japanese food</a> to <a href="http://www.blogto.com/eat_drink/2015/01/where_to_eat_poutine_pizza_in_toronto/">pizza</a>. Purists prefer to savour the flavours of hand-cut potatoes, squeaky curds and slow-simmered gravy unadorned; I say there's room for everyone.</p>

<p>Here is the best poutine in Toronto.</p>
